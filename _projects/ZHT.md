<p>This is an assignement made in the context of the heat transfer class for mechanical engineers at Cornell University.</p>
    
<p>This assignement allowed me to reflect on how to analyse multi-layer real world system, where losses are taken into account. It also provided a great illustration on the use of extended surfaces and their effectiveness in two different context. 
</p>

<p> Across all three problems, thinking with thermal resistance was one of the key approaches. It can be applied to any engineering system that deals with thermal exchanges, which is most of the existing engineering systems. I am particularly interested into quantum devices and aerospace systems, which are both directly concerned by maintaining specific temperatures, which makes this assignement and way of thinking directly relevant to them.</p>
